Question: Which element(s) is/are in the complement of set Q, denoted as \( Q^{\prime} \)? ![A diagram showing a Venn diagram of 2 circles. The left circle is for Set P and contains rabbit in its left part, rabbit & turtle in the intersection. The right circle is for set Q and contains parrot in its right part, turtle in the intersection. Outside of the circles it says fish.]() 

###
Answers:
(A) - rabbit 

(B) - rabbit & turtle 

(C) - \( \begin{array}{c}\text { parrot \& } \\ \text { fish }\end{array} \) 

(D) - turtle, parrot & fish 

###
Misconceptions:
(A) - Does not understand that elements outside both sets are included in the complement of one set. 

(B) - Misinterprets the complement as including elements from the intersection or one specific set. 

(C) - nan 

(D) - Confuses the intersection of sets with the definition of a complement, including elements that are only in one set.

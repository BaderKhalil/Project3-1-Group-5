###
Question: The following frequency table displays the number of books read by a group of students during the summer. What was the median number of books read? ![\begin{tabular}{|c|c|}
\hline Books Read & Frequency \\
\hline \( 0 \) & \( 4 \) \\
\hline \( 1 \) & \( 6 \\
\hline \( 2 \) & \( 5 \) \\
\hline \( 3 \) & \( 3 \) \\
\hline \( 4 \) & \( 2 \) \\
\hline
\end{tabular}]() 

###
Answers:
(A) - \( 2 \) 

(B) - \( 3 \) 

(C) - \( 5 \) 

(D) - \( 1 \) 

###
Misconceptions:
(A) - Believes the median is simply the middle number in the list without considering frequencies. 

(B) - Believes that the median is the number of students who read the most books rather than the quantitative middle of the dataset. 

(C) - Believes the median must be the highest frequency rather than finding the central value based on cumulative frequency. 

(D) - Believes the median is the least number in the dataset.

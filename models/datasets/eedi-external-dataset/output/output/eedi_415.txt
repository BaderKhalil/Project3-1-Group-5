Sarah claims \( \mathbf{7} \) is a term in the sequence.

Mike argues \( \mathbf{12} \) is a term in the sequence.

Who is correct? 

###
Answers:
(A) - Only Sarah

(B) - Only Mike 

(C) - Both Sarah and Mike 

(D) - Neither is correct 

###

(B) - Believes that adding numbers can result in values that don't adhere to the sequence's formula.

(C) - Thinks that if one term is in the sequence, then the other must also be.

(D) - Correctly identifies that neither value can be produced by the given sequence rule.

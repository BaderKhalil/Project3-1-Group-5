Question: Given the function \( \boldsymbol{y}=\mathbf{3}^{\boldsymbol{x}} \), find the value of \( y \) when \( x = 2/3 \). 

###

(A) - \( 9 \)

(C) - \( \sqrt{3} \) 

(D) - \( 3^{2/3} \)

(B) - Misunderstands the concept of exponentiation and applies the wrong root

(D) - Correctly represents the exponent but misinterprets the evaluation of \( 3^{2/3} \)

Question: Which of the following is a common factor of: \( 12x^3 + 16x^2 \)?

###
Answers:
(A) - \( 4 \)

(B) - \( 12x \)

(C) - \( 8x^2 \)

(D) - \( 2x^2 \)

###
Misconceptions:
(A) - Thinks any number that divides the coefficients can be considered a common factor without checking the variable part.

(B) - Overlooks that while \( 12x \) is part of one term, it is not a common factor of both terms.

(C) - Assumes a term from one part of the expression can be a common factor without considering its presence in both terms.

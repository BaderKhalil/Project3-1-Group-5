Question: A gardener plans to plant flowers in a rectangular flower bed that measures \( 8.4 \mathrm{~m} \) in length and \( 5.6 \mathrm{~m} \) in width. Which calculation will provide the best estimate of the area of the flower bed?

###
Answers:
(A) - \( 8 \times 6 \)

(B) - \( 8 \times 5 \)

(C) - \( 9 \times 6 \)

(D) - \( 8 \times 5.5 \)

###
Misconceptions:
(A) - Only rounds down and does not account for the numbers in between.

(B) - Rounds down and ignores the more precise estimate.

(C) - Rounds up the length incorrectly, leading to an inaccurate area.

(D) - Uses a decimal in multiplication, which can lead to confusion in estimation.

###
Question: What figure do you create by connecting the midpoints of the sides of a square? ![A square drawn on a grid]()

###
Answers:
(A) - Rectangle 

(B) - Trapezoid 

(C) - Triangle 

(D) - Parallelogram 

###
Misconceptions:
(A) - Does not understand that a rectangle requires only opposite sides to be equal and parallel, whereas all angles in this new figure are right angles.

(B) - Incorrectly believes that a trapezoid can be formed from midpoints of a square due to a lack of understanding about parallel sides.

(C) - Misunderstands that only three sides are connected instead of the four available.

(D) - Does not recognize that a parallelogram formed by midpoints has equal diagonals, aligning with properties of a square.

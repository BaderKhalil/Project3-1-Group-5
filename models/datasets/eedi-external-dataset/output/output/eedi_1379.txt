Question: Determine the distance \( (s) \) traveled by an object under uniform acceleration \( (a)=3.5 \mathrm{~ms}^{-2} \) with initial velocity \( (u)=1.2 \mathrm{~ms}^{-1} \) and time \( (t)=4.0 \mathrm{~s} \) using the equation \( s = ut + \frac{1}{2}at^2 \).

###
Answers:
(A) - \( \begin{array}{c}s = 1.2 \times 4.0 + \frac{1}{2} \times 3.5 \times 4.0^2 \\ s = 21.2 \mathrm{~m}\end{array} \) 

(B) - \( \begin{array}{c}s = 1.2 + 4.0 + 3.5 \\ s = 8.7 \mathrm{~m}\end{array} \) 

(C) - \( \begin{array}{c}s = 1.2 \times 4.0 + \frac{3.5}{2} \times 4.0^2 \\ s = 32.0 \mathrm{~m}\end{array} \) 

(D) - \( \begin{array}{c}s = 1.2 \times 4.0 + \frac{1}{2} \times 3.5 \times 4.0^2 \\ s = 33.2 \mathrm{~m}\end{array} \)

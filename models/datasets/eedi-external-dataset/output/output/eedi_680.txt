###
Question: Sarah and Mike are discussing expanding and simplifying expressions.

Sarah says \( 3(x + 5) + 2(x + 3) \equiv 5x + 15 \)

Mike says \( 3(x + 5) + 2(x + 3) \equiv 5x + 21 \)

Who is correct? 

###
Answers:
(A) - Only Sarah 

(B) - Only Mike 

(C) - Both Sarah and Mike 

(D) - Neither is correct 

###
Misconceptions:
(A) - Believes that constant terms are added incorrectly when simplifying the expression. 

(B) - Incorrectly adds the constants contributing to the total, not following the distributive property properly. 

(C) - Both would need to be correct for this response, but both actually made errors. 

(D) - Correctly recognizes that both have made mistakes in their calculations. 

###
Subject: Expanding and Simplifying Expressions
###
ConstructName: Expand expressions by applying the distributive property and combine like terms.
###
CorrectAnswer: D

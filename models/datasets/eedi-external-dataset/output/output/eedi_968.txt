Question: Given the equation
\[
y = 4 - 2x
\] fill in the missing value in the table below:
\begin{tabular}{|l|l|}
\hline\( x \) & \( \bigstar \) \\
\hline\( y \) & \( 0 \) \\
\hline
\end{tabular} What should replace the star? 

###
Answers:
(A) - \( 2 \)

(B) - \( -2 \)

(C) - \( 4 \)

(D) - \( 0 \)

###
Misconceptions:
(A) - There is a correct relationship identified between the variables.

(B) - Believes that substituting a negative value will result in a negative output for \( y \).

(C) - Incorrectly assumes that substituting a number simply mirrors the equation's constants without performing the calculation.

(D) - Thinks that if \( y \) equals \( 0 \), then \( x \) must also equal \( 0 \).

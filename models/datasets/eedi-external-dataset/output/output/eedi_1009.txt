Question: Sarah and Liam are solving the equation:
\( \frac{5y}{3} - 2 = 1.5 \)

Sarah claims a correct next step could be: \( \frac{5y}{3} = 3.5 \)

Liam suggests that a correct next step could be: \( 5y - 3 = 4.5 \)

Who is correct? 

###
Answers:
(A) - Only Sarah

(B) - Only Liam

(C) - Both Sarah and Liam

(D) - Neither is correct

###
Misconceptions:
(A) - Believes it's acceptable to manipulate the equation correctly by isolating the variable on one side. 

(B) - Thinks that when dealing with fractions, you can simply subtract from the numerator instead of dealing with the entire fraction appropriately. 

(C) - Incorrectly assumes that both methods used in the steps lead to valid manipulations of the equation.

(D) - Does not realize that moving terms between sides of the equation can lead to incorrect results if not done correctly.

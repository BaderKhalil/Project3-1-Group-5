Question: Sarah and John are discussing how to calculate the discount on an item.

Sarah claims that to find a \( 25\% \) discount on a price, you can simply divide the price by \( 4 \). 
John insists that to calculate a \( 25\% \) discount, you should subtract \( 25 \) from the original price and then divide by \( 4 \). 
Who is correct?

###
Answers:
(A) - Only Sarah

(B) - Only John 

(C) - Both Sarah and John 

(D) - Neither is correct 

###
Misconceptions:
(A) - Assumes that \( 25\% \) is equivalent to simply dividing by \( 4 \) without understanding the context of subtraction.

(B) - Misunderstands the process by believing subtraction from the original price is necessary before calculating the percentage. 

(C) - Incorrectly believes that both methods will yield the correct discount.

(D) - Fails to recognize how to appropriately apply percentage calculations.

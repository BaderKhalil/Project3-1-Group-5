Question: A group of students participated in a survey about their favorite types of fruit. The results are shown in the frequency table below. \begin{tabular}{|c|c|}
\hline Type of fruit & Frequency \\
\hline Apples & \( 5 \) \\
\hline Bananas & \( 3 \) \\
\hline Cherries & \( 7 \) \\
\hline Dates & \( 2 \) \\
\hline Elderberries & \( 4 \) \\
\hline
\end{tabular} How many students participated in the survey? 

###
Answers:
(A) - \( 15 \) 

(B) - \( 13 \) 

(C) - \( 10 \) 

(D) - \( 20 \) 

###
Misconceptions:
(A) - Correctly adds the frequencies but miscounts the total. 

(B) - Counts only some of the frequencies instead of all of them. 

(C) - Only considers the category with the highest frequency for total participation.

(D) - Doubles the result thinking to account for potential duplicates in preferences.

Question: Sarah and Mike are discussing the distribution of multiplication over addition. Sarah states \( 3(x + 4) = 3x + 12 \) while Mike claims \( 3(x + 4) = 3x + 4 \). Who is correct?

###
Answers:
(A) - Only Sarah 

(B) - Only Mike 

(C) - Both Sarah and Mike 

(D) - Neither is correct 

###
Misconceptions:
(A) - Correctly understands the distribution of multiplication 

(B) - Misses the concept of multiplying all terms inside the parentheses 

(C) - Assumes that only the first term is multiplied 

(D) - Misunderstands both distribution and the equality of expressions

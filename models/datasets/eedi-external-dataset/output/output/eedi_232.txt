###
Question: Sarah and Jake are discussing multiples.

Sarah claims that \( 4 \) is a common multiple of \( 8 \) and \( 12 \).

Jake argues that \( 6 \) is a common multiple of \( 8 \) and \( 12 \).

Who is correct?

###
Answers:
(A) - Only Sarah 

(B) - Only Jake 

(C) - Both Sarah and Jake 

(D) - Neither is correct 

###
Misconceptions:
(A) - Does not understand what a common multiple is or fails to recognize that \(4\) is indeed not a multiple of \(8\).

(B) - Believes that any number greater than the largest number in the list can be a common multiple rather than checking for multiples.

(C) - Does not know how to identify common multiples correctly for two numbers.

(D) - Does not understand the definition of a common multiple and fails at understanding the relationship between the numbers.

###
Question: Which expression correctly represents the following description?
"Add four to \( x \) and then divide by two."

###
Answers:
(A) - \( \frac{x + 4}{2} \)

(B) - \( \frac{4 + x}{2} \)

(C) - \( \frac{x}{2} + 4 \)

(D) - \( 2(x + 4) \)

###
Misconceptions:
(A) - nan

(B) - Misunderstands the order of operations and implies adding 4 before applying division.

(C) - Incorrectly applies distribution of division over addition, altering the intended operation.

(D) - Ignores the division by two and instead mistakenly multiplies the expression by two.
